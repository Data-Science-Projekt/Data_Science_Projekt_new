GARCH Modeling

To model conditional variance, we use GARCH(1,1) models:

  • ARCH Effect: Past shocks influence current volatility
  • GARCH Effect: Persistence of volatility over time
  • Leverage Effect: Negative returns increase volatility more strongly

Volatility Regimes

Low Volatility

Stable market phases with low fluctuations. Typical for sustained uptrends.

High Volatility

Crisis periods with strong price movements. Often associated with market crashes or external shocks.

Key Finding

Volatility occurs in clusters — periods of high fluctuations tend to follow each other, which can be well captured by GARCH models.
